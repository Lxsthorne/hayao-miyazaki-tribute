BIOGRAPHY

Back Top

Hayao Miyazaki was born on January 5, 1941 in the midst of the Second World War. He was born in the town of Akebono-cho of the Bunkyo ward of Tokyo. Hayao's father Katsuji Miyazaki was the head of Miyazaki Airplane, a company that manufactured parts for fighter planes during World War II.

Miyazaki was barely four years old when the United States deployed bombs over Hiroshima, Nagasaki, and Utsunomiya. Shortly thereafter, his mother was stricken with spinal tuberculosis. These early life events would go on to influence Miyazaki's storytelling, which often depicts children facing harrowing and near impossible problems caused by powers beyond their control.

His career began in 1963 at Toei Animation where he worked as an in-between artist. Within five years, he advanced to the role of chief animator and concept artist. He eventually collborated on what would be his first feature film The Great Adventure of Horus, Prince of the Sun with acclaimed animator Isao Takahata. The two would go on to collaborate for many more films and become lifelong friends.

In June of 1985, Takahata, Miyazaki, and several other animators would found Studio Ghibli. Many of his most famous films would come from here. Miyazaki would also go on to direct nearly two dozen of its films. Miyazaki's final feature film wasThe Wind Rises, a story about World War II fighterplane designer Jiro Horikoshi. The film received critical acclaim and was named Animation of the Year at the 37th Japan Academy Prize. Shortly thereafter, Miyazaki announced his retirement from film production in September of 2013.

Today, you can find Hayao Miyazaki working on manga, short films, or fixing the displays at Studio Ghibli in Japan. And a little bird somewhere might know something about a possible new final film.
